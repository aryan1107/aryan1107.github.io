# Privacy Policy — Quad Words

This explains what information **Quad Words** ("the app") handles, how it
is used, and what choices you have. Plain English where possible. If
anything is unclear, see the [support page](support.html) for ways to
reach us.

Quad Words is offered free of charge with optional in-app purchases. By
using Quad Words you agree to the practices described here.

## Summary

Quad Words is **anonymous** — no sign-up, no username, no email. We do
not create or store an account for you. Your puzzle progress lives on
your device. The only data we share with third parties is a **device
identifier (Device ID)** — used for app analytics, advertising in the
free version, and app functionality. You can disable personalized
advertising on iOS or Android at any time.

## What we collect

We try to collect as little as possible.

- **On-device game data** — daily and practice puzzle progress,
  streaks, win/loss history, preferences (theme, sound, haptics,
  reminder time), and onboarding state. This stays on your device.
  We do not upload it to a server tied to your identity.
- **Device ID (identifier)** — a non-personal identifier from your
  device used to:
  - measure aggregate app analytics (which features get used),
  - serve advertising in the free version (subject to your platform
    tracking choice — see "Advertising controls" below),
  - support core app functionality (e.g., associating a purchase
    with the device that bought it).
  This is the data type listed as "Identifiers" on the App Store
  privacy label and as "Device or other IDs" on the Google Play
  Data Safety section.
- **Purchase & subscription receipts** — purchases are processed by
  the platform you bought through (App Store on iOS, Google Play on
  Android). We never see your name, billing address, or card
  details. A purchase receipt is shared with our third-party
  subscription service to validate the purchase, restore it on
  other devices, and check whether Premium is active. The receipt
  is keyed to your Apple ID or Google account, not to any account
  we maintain.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — if the app crashes, anonymous device
  data (model, OS version, app version, error stack trace, time of
  error) is sent to a third-party diagnostic tool to help us fix
  bugs. No personal data is included.
- **App activity (aggregate)** — anonymous usage events (e.g.,
  "puzzle completed", "settings opened", screen views) sent to a
  third-party analytics service. Used in aggregate to understand
  which features are used. Not tied to any account we maintain.

## What we don't collect

- **No accounts.** We do not create accounts. There is no username,
  no email, no password.
- **No personal contact info.** We don't ask for, collect, or store
  your name, email, phone number, or address.
- **No chat history.** Quad Words has an optional in-app chat helper.
  Your messages are sent to a third-party AI service to generate a
  response and are **not retained by us**. We don't keep a
  server-side copy of your conversations. (The third-party AI
  service has its own privacy policy that governs whatever it does
  with the prompt while it's processing it.)
- **No selling.** We don't sell your data. Ever.
- **No iCloud / photos / contacts.** We don't read anything outside
  the app.

## Advertising controls

The free version of Quad Words is supported by ads. To keep the app
free and ads relevant, our ad partners may use your device's
advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS, Advertising ID on Android).

The app works identically whether or not personalized ads are
enabled. There is no penalty, locked content, or feature loss based
on your tracking choice. If you upgrade to Premium, ads are removed
entirely and no ad-related identifiers are shared.

### iOS — App Tracking Transparency

Apple requires apps to ask before any cross-app tracking. The first
time you set up Quad Words (or shortly after, if you already had it
installed before ads shipped), you'll see Apple's standard **App
Tracking Transparency** prompt:

- **Allow tracking** — ads may be personalized to your interests.
- **Ask not to track** — no advertising identifier is shared. Ads
  still appear, but won't be personalized.

You can change this at any time:

- **iOS-wide:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking
- **Per app:** Settings → Quad Words → Allow Tracking

### Android — Advertising ID

On Android, ads are served using your device's Advertising ID. You
can reset it or opt out of personalized advertising at any time:

- **Reset / delete:** Settings → Privacy → Ads (or Settings →
  Google → Ads, depending on your device)
- **Opt out of personalization:** turn on "Opt out of Ads
  Personalization" in the same screen.

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United
Kingdom, or Switzerland, you'll see an in-app consent prompt the
first time the app loads ads, asking whether you want personalized
or non-personalized ads. You can change that choice later from the
app's settings or by clearing app data.

## Third-party services

A small number of trusted providers help run the app. We share with
them only the information they need to do their job, and they are
bound not to use it for anything else:

- **Payment & subscription processing** — to validate purchases,
  restore them on other devices, and check Premium status.
- **Advertising services** — to display ads in the free version and
  report aggregate performance.
- **Diagnostic & crash reporting** — to detect and fix bugs.
- **Analytics** — to understand, in aggregate, how the app is used.
- **AI chat** — the optional in-app chat helper sends your messages
  to an AI service for processing. Messages are not retained by us.

Each of these services has its own privacy policy and may collect
information as part of providing its service.

## Notifications

If you allow notifications during onboarding, the app may schedule a
single gentle reminder if you've been away for a day or more. These
reminders are scheduled and delivered locally by your device —
nothing is sent from a server, and we do not track whether you
opened them.

You can turn them off at any time:

- **iOS:** Settings → Notifications → Quad Words
- **Android:** Settings → Apps → Quad Words → Notifications

## Saving images

If you tap "Save Image" on a result screen, the app asks permission
to save the image to your device. The image is saved locally on
your device (Photo Library on iOS, Photos / Pictures folder on
Android). We do not read or upload anything from your library.

## How long things are kept

- **Local game data** — stays on your device until you delete the
  app. Removing the app erases everything.
- **Purchase records** — may be kept by the App Store, Google Play,
  and our third-party subscription service as long as required for
  accounting or legal reasons.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — typically kept for a limited period
  (around 90 days) by the diagnostic provider, then deleted.
- **Analytics events** — retained for a limited period by the
  analytics provider (typically up to 14 months), then aggregated
  or deleted.
- **Advertising identifiers** — retained by ad partners according
  to their own policies. You can reset them any time from your
  device's settings (see "Advertising controls" above).

Because we do not maintain an account for you, there is little on
our side to "delete" — uninstalling the app removes your data on
your device, and you can reset your advertising identifier in your
device settings at any time.

## Data deletion requests

If you'd like us to request deletion of any data held by our
third-party providers (analytics, diagnostic, advertising, or
subscription services) that may be associated with your device,
please contact us through the [support page](support.html) with
the subject line **"Data Deletion Request"**. Where possible,
include:

- the platform you use (iOS or Android),
- your in-app User ID if you can find it (Settings → About), and
- the approximate date of your last use.

We'll forward the request to the relevant providers and confirm
back to you, typically within 30 days. There is no in-app account
to delete because we don't create one.
