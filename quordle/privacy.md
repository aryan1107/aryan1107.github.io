# Privacy Policy — Quordle

This explains what information **Quordle** ("the app") handles, how it
is used, and what choices you have. Plain English where possible. If
anything is unclear, write to me at
[app+quordle@brutusai.com](mailto:app+quordle@brutusai.com).

Quordle is offered free of charge with optional in-app purchases. By
using Quordle you agree to the practices described here.

## Summary

I do not sell personal information. An account is not required to play
— most of your game data lives on your device. A few trusted services
help with sign-in, sync, payments, ads, and bug reports. You can
disable personalized ads at any time on iOS, and you can ask for
account data to be deleted by emailing me.

## What I collect

Only what is needed to make the app work:

- **On-device game data** — daily and practice puzzle progress,
  streaks, win/loss history, preferences (theme, sound, haptics,
  reminder time), and onboarding state. This stays on your device
  unless you sign in.
- **Account & sync data (optional)** — if you sign in, a non-personal
  account identifier, your stats / progress / Premium entitlement, and
  the sign-in identifier you used (for example a private Sign in with
  Apple relay address) are stored so your progress can sync across
  your devices.
- **Purchase & subscription data** — purchases are processed by the
  App Store, so I never see your name, billing address, or card
  details. A receipt is shared with the subscription service to
  validate purchases, restore them across devices, and check whether
  Premium is currently active.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — if the app crashes, anonymous device
  data (model, OS version, app version, error stack trace, anonymous
  install id, time of error) is collected to help fix bugs.
- **Advertising identifier (free version only)** — depending on your
  iOS tracking choice, the device advertising identifier (IDFA) may be
  shared with advertising partners along with limited contextual data
  about the placement. If you do not allow tracking, no advertising
  identifier is shared. Either way, ads still appear in the free
  version — the free version stays free.
- **Aggregate usage** — anonymous, non-identifying data about which
  features are used and how the app performs.

## What I don't do

- I don't sell your personal data. Ever.
- I don't build advertising profiles myself.
- I don't read your iCloud, your photos, or anything outside the app.
- I don't send marketing emails.

## Third-party services

A small number of trusted providers help run the app. I share with
them only the information they need to do their job, and they are
bound not to use it for anything else:

- **Authentication & cloud storage** — to let you sign in and sync
  stats and progress between devices.
- **Payment & subscription processing** — to validate purchases,
  restore them on other devices, and check Premium status.
- **Advertising services** — to display ads in the free version and
  report aggregate performance.
- **Diagnostic & crash reporting** — to detect and fix bugs.
- **Analytics** — to understand, in aggregate, how the app is used.

Each of these services has its own privacy policy and may collect
information as part of providing its service.

## Advertising and App Tracking Transparency (iOS)

The free version is ad-supported. To keep ads relevant and the app
free, advertising partners can — with your permission — use your
device's advertising identifier.

On iOS, Apple requires apps to ask before any cross-app tracking. You
will see Apple's standard **App Tracking Transparency** prompt:

- **Allow tracking** — ads may be personalized to your interests.
- **Ask not to track** — no advertising identifier is shared. Ads
  still appear, but won't be personalized.

The app works identically either way. There is no penalty, locked
content, or feature loss based on your tracking choice.

You can change this at any time:

- **iOS-wide:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking
- **Per app:** Settings → Quordle → Allow Tracking

If you upgrade to Premium, ads are removed and no ad-related
identifiers are shared.

## Notifications

If you allow notifications during onboarding, the app may schedule a
single gentle reminder if you've been away for a day or more. These
reminders are scheduled and delivered locally by your device — nothing
is sent from a server, and I do not track whether you opened them.

You can turn them off at any time in **Settings → Notifications →
Quordle**.

## Photo Library

If you tap "Save Image" on a result screen, the app asks permission to
save the image to your Photo Library. The image is saved locally on
your device. I do not read or upload anything from your library.

## How long things are kept

- **Local game data** — stays on your device until you delete the app.
- **Synced account data** — kept while your account is active. You
  can ask for it to be deleted at any time.
- **Purchase records** — may be kept as long as required for
  accounting or legal reasons.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — typically kept for a limited period
  by the diagnostic provider, then deleted.
- **Aggregate analytics** — non-identifying, may be kept indefinitely.

## Your choices and rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

- **Access** the personal data I hold about you.
- **Correct** anything that is inaccurate.
- **Delete** your data ("right to be forgotten").
- **Object to** or **restrict** certain processing.
- **Withdraw consent** at any time.
- **Port** your data in a machine-readable format.
- **Lodge a complaint** with your local data protection authority.

EU/UK (GDPR) and California (CCPA / CPRA) residents may have
additional rights. To exercise any of these, email me at the address
below.

## Account & data deletion

To delete your account data, email me. I'll respond within a
reasonable time, typically 30 days. Deleting your account does not
affect:

- Records of past purchases that I'm required to keep by law.
- Aggregated, non-identifying analytics.
- Local data on your device — you can delete this by deleting the app.
