# Privacy Policy — Worldle

This explains what information **Worldle** ("the app") handles, how
it is used, and what choices you have. Plain English where possible.
If anything is unclear, see the [support page](support.html) for ways
to reach us.

Worldle is offered free of charge with optional in-app purchases. By
using Worldle you agree to the practices described here.

## Summary

You can play Worldle **without an account** — solo puzzles, daily
challenges, streaks, and widgets all work anonymously. If you want
to use the multiplayer features (duels, friend invites, leaderboards,
custom maps, the live chat that runs during a match), you can
optionally **sign in with your Apple ID or Google account**. Signing
in gives you a persistent identity, a friends list, and cross-device
sync.

We share a small amount of data with trusted third-party providers
so the app can function (authentication, push delivery, payments,
ads, analytics, crash reporting, AI chat, and content moderation).
You can delete your account and all the data tied to it at any time
from inside the app.

## What we collect

We try to collect as little as possible. What we keep depends on how
you use the app.

### When you play anonymously (no sign-in)

- **On-device game data** — daily and Daily Flags puzzle progress,
  streaks, win/loss history, preferences (theme, sound, haptics,
  reminder time), and onboarding state. This stays on your device.
- **Anonymous identifier** — when the app first launches it creates
  an anonymous user ID through our authentication provider. It is
  not tied to your name, email, or device serial; it just lets the
  app sync some features (like Atlas progress backups) without
  asking you to sign up. The anonymous ID lives on your device and
  on our servers.
- **Device ID (identifier)** — a non-personal identifier from your
  device used to:
  - measure aggregate app analytics (which features get used),
  - serve advertising in the free version (subject to your iOS
    tracking choice — see ATT below),
  - support core app functionality (e.g., associating a purchase
    with the device that bought it).
  This is the data type listed under "Identifiers" in the App Store
  privacy nutrition label.
- **Purchase & subscription receipts** — purchases are processed by
  the App Store. We never see your name, billing address, or card
  details. A purchase receipt is shared with our subscription
  verification provider to validate the purchase, restore it on
  other devices, and check whether Premium is active.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — if the app crashes, anonymous device
  data (model, OS version, app version, error stack trace, time of
  error) is sent to a third-party diagnostic tool to help us fix
  bugs. No personal data is included.
- **Aggregate session analytics** — anonymized session recordings
  and event metrics through a third-party analytics provider, which
  help us see which screens people get stuck on. No keystrokes from
  text fields are captured. Recordings are tied to the anonymous ID
  above, not to your real identity.

### When you sign in (Apple or Google)

In addition to the above, signing in adds a small amount of
identifying information so we can give you an actual account:

- **Account identity** — your Apple user ID (or Google user ID),
  whichever you used. If you chose to share it during Sign In with
  Apple, your name; otherwise just the opaque identifier Apple
  gives us.
- **Email address** — if you signed in with Google, the email tied
  to that account. If you signed in with Apple, only the email or
  relay address that you chose to share at sign-in time. We do not
  use this address for marketing.
- **Profile** — a handle (you pick this), display name, country
  flag, level, ladder rating, and (optional) avatar image you
  uploaded.
- **Social graph** — your friends list, sent and received friend
  requests, invite codes you generated or redeemed.
- **Match history** — duel, battle royale, and custom-map results
  (scores, opponents, dates), plus the live multiplayer replay
  data needed to show the recap screen.
- **Custom content** — any maps or live challenges you create
  while signed in.

### When you turn on push notifications

- **Push token** — when you allow notifications, your device's
  push token is uploaded to our backend so the server can send you
  the notification types you've enabled (friend request, friend
  accepted, friend challenge, daily nudges, streak warnings, etc.).
  Each type can be toggled individually inside the app. Push tokens
  are device-specific, not personal — they identify the install,
  not you.

### When you use the contact picker or photo picker

- **Contact picker (friend invites by SMS)** — we use Apple's
  standard contact picker. iOS shows the picker in its own process;
  the app never sees your full address book and never asks for
  Contacts access. We only receive the one contact you tap. The
  resulting SMS is composed inside Apple's own iMessage UI — we do
  not see the message body or whether you sent it.
- **Photo picker (avatar upload)** — we use Apple's standard photo
  picker. iOS shows the picker in its own process; the app never
  sees your full photo library and never asks for Photos access. We
  only receive the one image you tap, which is uploaded to our
  servers as your avatar.

### When you use in-app chat or AI features

- **Live multiplayer chat** — short text messages exchanged with
  the other players in your match. Messages pass through an
  automated content moderation service to filter abuse before they
  reach other players. Messages are kept only for the lifetime of
  the match (replay retention window) and then removed.
- **AI helper chat** — the optional "Ask AI" tab sends your
  messages to a third-party AI service to generate a response.
  Messages are not retained by us. The third-party AI service has
  its own privacy policy that governs whatever it does with the
  prompt while it's processing it.
- **Avatar moderation** — avatar images you upload pass through an
  automated content moderation service before being published, so
  the leaderboard doesn't surface inappropriate content.

## What we don't collect

- **No passwords.** Sign-in is handled entirely by Apple or Google
  (or stays anonymous). We never see or store a password.
- **No address book / no full photo library access.** The contact
  and photo pickers run in iOS's own process — we only get the
  single item you tap.
- **No camera / microphone / location.** Worldle does not ask for
  any of these.
- **No chat history (AI helper).** Your AI chat messages are not
  retained by us.
- **No selling.** We don't sell your data. Ever.

## App integrity

Worldle uses Apple's device integrity check (App Attest) plus an
app-integrity verification service on our backend to confirm that
requests really come from the unmodified Worldle app on a real iOS
device. This protects the leaderboard and multiplayer system from
bots and tampered clients. The integrity check does not collect
personal data; it just produces a cryptographic assertion that says
"yes, this is a genuine copy of the app."

## Advertising and App Tracking Transparency (iOS)

The free version of Worldle is supported by ads. Ads are served by
**Google AdMob** (Google's mobile advertising network). To keep the
app free and ads relevant, Google may use your device's advertising
identifier (IDFA on iOS).

We do **not** choose, review, or control the specific ads that
Google decides to show inside the app. Google's network auctions
each ad slot in real time based on its own targeting and inventory.
If you see an ad that is offensive, misleading, or otherwise
inappropriate, please report it through Apple's "Report a Concern"
on the ad itself — that flag reaches Google, who can act on it.
The developer has no ability to remove or block individual ads.

On iOS, Apple requires apps to ask before any cross-app tracking.
The first time you set up Worldle (or shortly after, if you already
had it installed before ads shipped), you'll see Apple's standard
**App Tracking Transparency** prompt:

- **Allow tracking** — ads may be personalized to your interests.
- **Ask not to track** — no advertising identifier is shared. Ads
  still appear, but won't be personalized.

The app works identically either way. There is no penalty, locked
content, or feature loss based on your tracking choice.

You can change this at any time:

- **iOS-wide:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking
- **Per app:** Settings → Worldle → Allow Tracking

If you upgrade to Premium, ads are removed entirely and no
ad-related identifiers are shared.

## Third-party services

A small number of trusted providers help run the app. Each has its
own privacy policy that governs what they do with the information
they receive.

- **Authentication provider** — handles anonymous IDs, Sign In with
  Apple, and Google sign-in; manages your session token.
- **Push notification service** — delivers notifications to your
  device.
- **Backend infrastructure provider** — stores multiplayer state,
  friends, leaderboards, match replays, custom maps, and avatar
  images.
- **App Store / StoreKit** — processes purchases and subscriptions.
- **Subscription verification service** — verifies the App Store
  receipt for your purchase and tells the app whether Premium is
  active.
- **Google AdMob** — Google's mobile advertising network displays
  ads in the free version and reports aggregate performance. Ad
  selection and targeting are entirely Google's — the developer has
  no control over which specific ads appear.
- **AI service** — powers the "Ask AI" helper chat.
- **Content moderation service** — screens live multiplayer chat
  messages and uploaded avatar images.
- **Analytics provider** — anonymized session analytics and event
  tracking to help us understand which screens are confusing.
- **Diagnostic & crash reporting provider** — detects and reports
  crashes so we can fix them.
- **Map and street-imagery providers** — the geography scenes you
  play come from third-party map and street-imagery providers. They
  run inside the app like any other map view.

## Notifications

You'll be asked whether to allow notifications during onboarding
(and can change your mind any time). When you allow them, the app
uploads your device's push token to our backend so the server can
send notifications for the categories you've enabled.

Examples of what we may send:

- A daily nudge when a fresh daily challenge unlocks (optional).
- A streak warning a couple of hours before your streak would
  break (optional).
- Friend requests, friend acceptances, and friend challenges.
- Duel invites and live match events.

You can:

- Turn notifications off entirely in **Settings → Notifications →
  Worldle**.
- Toggle individual categories inside the app under **Profile →
  Notifications**.

If you sign out or delete your account, the push token is removed
from our backend and we will not send you any more notifications.

## Your account and your data

You are in control of your data. From inside the app you can:

- **Stay anonymous.** Just don't sign in; everything stays on the
  device.
- **Sign in or sign out.** Profile → Sign In (or Sign Out).
  Signing out clears the cached session on this device.
- **Change your handle, display name, or country** at any time
  from Profile.
- **Manage notifications** by category in the in-app notification
  preferences.
- **Delete your account.** Profile → Settings → Delete Account.
  This removes your profile, handle, friendships, match history,
  custom maps, avatar image, push token, and all other personal
  data tied to your account from our backend. It also signs you
  out on every device. Action is irreversible.

If you only want to remove the app from your phone, deleting the
app erases the on-device data. Server-side account data stays
until you also use the Delete Account flow above (or contact us via
the support page to do it on your behalf).

## How long things are kept

- **Local game data** — stays on your device until you delete the
  app. Removing the app erases everything on the device.
- **Account & profile data** — kept while your account exists.
  Account deletion removes it.
- **Match history / replays** — kept for a limited window so the
  recap and "recent matches" screens work, then pruned. Deleted
  with your account when you delete it.
- **Custom maps you authored** — kept while your account exists,
  unless you delete them individually.
- **Push tokens** — kept while you have notifications enabled and
  are signed in. Removed on sign-out, account deletion, or when
  you disable notifications.
- **Live multiplayer chat** — kept only for the active match's
  replay retention window, then deleted.
- **AI helper chat** — not retained by us.
- **Purchase records** — may be kept by the App Store and our
  subscription verification service as long as required for
  accounting or legal reasons.
- **Diagnostic & crash data** — typically kept for a limited period
  by the diagnostic provider, then deleted.
- **Aggregate analytics** — non-identifying, may be kept
  indefinitely.
